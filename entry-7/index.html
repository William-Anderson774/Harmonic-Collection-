VIA57 WEST, Hells Kitchen, Manhattan



VIA57WEST, or 625 West 57th street in Manhattan is a whole-block skyscraper along the Westside Highway and Riverside Park. The stricking triangular architecute, designed by Bjarke Ingels Group (BIG), encompasses the concept of a "courtscraper," or the combination of classical European courtyard architecture and the vertical density of New York super tall structures. With its tetrahedral and pyramidal 3 facades, the vessel stands out amoung the other buildings near it, using glass and aluminum siding, to reflect the light and water of the near-by Hudson and creates an optical "gateway" to motorist driving south along the highway.




The building and the engineering that allowed for it to be built have won serveral awards, most prodominently the 2016 International Highrise Award for best new skyscraper. Additionally, the building has been praised for its unique design and that its one of a handful of LEED Platinum certified residential buildings in New York City, meaning it meets the highest standards for energy efficiency and sustainability.

IHA Award for top skyscraper (2016).



Facts and Figures
- The building is 467 feet tall with 35 floors and contains 709 residential units.
- The building was completed in 2016 and took 4 years to construct.
- The building was designed by Bjarke Ingels Group (BIG) and developed by The Durst Organization.
- The building's unique shape allows for 80% of the units to have views of either the Hudson River or Central Park.
- The building includes amenities such as a fitness center, a swimming pool, and a rooftop terrace.
- The building's design was inspired by the shape of a tetrahedron and the concept of a "courtscraper."
