116 West 32nd Street (Gimbles Department Store Skybridge), Chelsea, Manhattan


Built in 1846, 43 Willow Place stands as one of five adjoining Greek Revival townhouses known collectively as Colonnade Row in the Willowtown section of Brooklyn Heights. This architectural ensemble is celebrated for its harmonious proportions and its continuous wooden portico, supported by tall, square columns that span the facades of all five homes. The unified portico is the defining feature, giving the row an elegant, temple-like presence that elevates what might otherwise have been modest middle-class housing into a striking architectural statement. The Greek Revival style dominated American domestic architecture in the mid-nineteenth century, inspired by admiration for ancient Greek democracy and ideals of balance and order. At 43 Willow Place, these influences are expressed through the crisp geometry of the columns, the restrained entablature, and the strong horizontal emphasis of the roofline. The materials—painted wood and brick—reflect both the craftsmanship and the accessible elegance of the era. Over time, the interior spaces have been modernized, but the exterior composition remains remarkably intact, offering a rare glimpse into the architectural ambitions of early Brooklyn Heights. The house, along with its neighbors, is part of the Brooklyn Heights Historic District, the first designated historic district in New York City. Together, these structures capture a key moment in Brooklyn’s urban development—when refinement and classical form were used to express civic pride and cultural aspiration. Today, 43 Willow Place continues to embody the timeless grace and disciplined beauty of the Greek Revival tradition.
