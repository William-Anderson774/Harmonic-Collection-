190 3rd Ave, Scheffel Hall, Gramercy, Manhattan



Although this seems like a rather unimreppessive 4 story townhouse on a main avenue, but it stands as a reprepresentation of the German community in the area and has strong ties to the culutural identy of Germany through it's architecture. The building, designed by Henry Allen Weber and Hubert Drosser in 1894, is meant to show the charm and spirit of German as a culutural symbol and stood for a long time as German resturant and beer garden and later in it's life an aethetlic club and now residences. The facade is modeled after a 17th-century Castle in Germany called the Heidelberg Castle. After hosting the famouse jazz club- Fat Tuesdays, a pilates studio and a pizza shop, the building was added to the Landmark Preservation List in 1997.


Photo of Sheffel Hall taken in 2017.



Some Facts and Figures about the Building:

- The building was designed by Henry Allen Weber and Hubert Drosser in 1894.

- The building was originally used as a German beer hall and restaurant called Scheffel Hall, later an athletic club and jazz club called Fat Tuesdays.

- The building was added to the New York City Landmarks Preservation Commission's list of landmarks in 1997.

- When the jazz club was open, famous musicians would join the resident band and play monday nights to the neighborhood and college students. Performers like Eddie Van Halen, Bob Dylan and Billy Idol all graced the basement stage.

- The building is located in the old Gramercy neighborhood of Manhattan, once a flourishing German Immegrant neighborhood known as Kleindeutschland.
