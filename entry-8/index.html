56 Leonard, Tribeca, Manhattan



57 Leonard Steet, or more locally refered to as the "Jenga Building," stands out amung the lower buildings of Tribeca and the Village. Visable throughout much of the Manhattan, the Jenga Building is unique in its cantelievered floors and prodruding balconies. Often critizied for its installment in a historical district, the structues "avant-garde" ambitions have been called into question since its inception.




Recently, the building has been the focus of many controversies, from the instalment of Anish Kapoor's 2012 "Untitiled" reflective bean to the highprofile suicide of Bed Bath and Beyond's CFO Gustavo Arnal, but stays steadfast as a desireable residence in Lower Manhattan.


Facts and Figures
- Nicknamed the Jenga building due to its cantelivered "blocks" and protrusions, with the designers aiming to have each block as conceptual "houses stacked in the sky."
- The building was completed in 2017 and took 8 years to construct.
- The building was designed by Herzog & de Meuron.
- At 827 feet tall, it stands as the tallest residential tower in the neighborhood.
- Contrary to the original renderings of the building, roughly 80% of the building is unremarkable and only the top 11 floors have the true "Jenga" effect.
- 57 Leonard got so tall due to the local zoning and the transfer of air-rights from the near-by Law School to this property.
- Anish Kapoor has a version of his iconic "Cloud Gate" ( the bean scultpture) at the base of this property. He also lived in a $17.8m appartment in this structure.
